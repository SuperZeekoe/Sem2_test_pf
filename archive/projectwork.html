Project work

For the project I’ve taken the de facto leadership role, mostly because I want to keep things moving forward and progress. This mostly comes down to keeping communication going and trying to create a short plan of action every time we’re working on the project.

In the project plan I’ve worked on the Risk Assessment, planning (gantt chart), and deliverables. Besides that I’ve delivered my input in the research methods, research main- and sub-questions, and the interview. After the initial project plan write-up, I’ve also read through it all and edited it where necessary to have a more coherent document. During the beforementioned interview I was present and taking notes.

We’re currently in the process of going through the research methods and together with Luc, we’ve done the competitor analysis. We found eight different artists/ art collectives websites and started writing down what each of them have that makes them stand out. Some were more focussed on actually selling art, others were focussed on showcasing their creations.

Design wise we’ve found a wide variety of different style choices, ranging from very minimalistic and “luxurious”, to more basic or colourful designs. Looking at these websites we found some definite no-go’s for our project website going forward and also some things we could try to implement ourselves.

We created a ‘Strengths & Weaknesses’ comparison chart once we felt like we collected enough data. The conclusion we came to, was that in order for our website to be a success, we’ll need to have the art as main view point on the website at all times. Some websites had very lacklustre UI/UX, so that is another thing we need to thoroughly do user-testing for once we’re at that stage of the project. Besides that, Erion seems to have a wide price range for his artwork, making it very accessible, which we should showcase as well.

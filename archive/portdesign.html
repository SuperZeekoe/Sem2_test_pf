Portfolio Design

My portfolio design started before Semester 2 kicked off. I found a really cool tutorial on Youtube, based on a portfolio website of a front end developer/designer. The core part being the moving image track. After following along with the guide, it didn’t work so I dived into the codepen provided and tweaked some things till it worked. I’ve added upon it slightly design wise and made the image themselves links, as well as layering a centre title on top of them.

The website isn’t completely done yet, there are still numerous things I want to improve upon. A side-ways scrolling function, maybe a one-pager. But for now I’ve separated everything into different pages.

Design wise I’m trying to keep a minimal approach, with accents referring to my logo. The colours I’ve chosen are yellow and red, with white and dark grey for text/background colour. Going into a somewhat calming feeling, with clear visuals. I’m trying to keep everything in a size so pages won’t have to be scrolled. Personally I prefer it that way, but this is something I want to add upon later, making it a more interactive one-pager, so you’d never leave the frontpage, but instead content will be brought to the user when clicking on a link.

I’ve gone through multiple iterations of design, while keeping the initial image-track as the centrepiece. For the content pages I’ve gone through multiple looks, with and without background, different colour accents, different layouts. In the end I settled on a straight forward design, top-down content -> key points -> direct links to work.

I felt like this would be the simplest approach and most clear for people visiting the website to find what they need.
